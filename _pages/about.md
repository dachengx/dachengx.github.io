I am a Ph.D. candidate in the [Department of Physics](https://www.physics.columbia.edu/) at [Columbia University](https://www.columbia.edu/), advised by **[Prof. Elena Aprile](https://www.physics.columbia.edu/content/elena-aprile)**. I am a member of the **[XENON Collaboration](https://xenonexperiment.org/)**, working at the intersection of neutrino physics and dark matter direct detection.

My research centers on the XENONnT experiment at the [Laboratori Nazionali del Gran Sasso (LNGS)](https://www.lngs.infn.it/en). I led the search for [coherent elastic neutrino–nucleus scattering (CEνNS) from solar ⁸B neutrinos](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.133.191002) and for [light dark matter](https://inspirehep.net/literature/2841348). This work achieved the first measurement of the ["neutrino fog"](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.127.251802), a long-anticipated irreducible background for next-generation dark matter searches. I have served as an **Analysis Coordinator** and as convener of the Computing and Analysis Tools Group within XENONnT.

Previously, I received my Bachelor of Engineering from the [Department of Engineering Physics, Tsinghua University](https://www.ep.tsinghua.edu.cn/en/), where I worked with [Prof. Fei Gao](https://www.phys.tsinghua.edu.cn/phyen/info/1063/1353.htm) and [Prof. Litao Yang](https://www.ep.tsinghua.edu.cn/en/info/1008/1112.htm) on [the conceptual design of a reactor neutrino detector](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.110.072011).

I have broad interests spanning physics, engineering, and computation, and I enjoy exploring ideas beyond my primary research. The **[Explorations](/explorations/)** page presents a selection of projects and is continuously updated.
